Welcome to Dalhousie

Explore the serene hill station and its beautiful attractions, a perfect blend of natural beauty and colonial charm. ❄️

Panchpula (Five Bridges)

Panchpula is a picturesque picnic spot featuring a mesmerizing waterfall and five small bridges over natural streams. It is a memorial dedicated to the great freedom fighter, Sardar Ajit Singh. It's perfect for short hikes and relaxing by the water.

Get Directions

St. John's Church

This historic colonial church, situated on the beautiful Mall Road, is a major landmark. Built in 1863, it showcases classic Victorian architecture and provides a peaceful retreat from the bustle of the town. Don't miss the rare photographs inside.

View History

Kalatop Wildlife Sanctuary

Located a short drive from the town, **Kalatop** offers stunning views, dense deodar and fir forests, and a chance to spot Himalayan wildlife. It is an ideal place for a quiet nature walk or trekking, especially the scenic route to Khajjiar.

Plan Trek

Khajjiar (Mini Switzerland)

Often referred to as the "**Mini Switzerland of India**," Khajjiar is a small picturesque plateau nestled in the foothills of the Dhauladhar range. It is famous for its lush green meadows, dense cedar forests, and the peaceful Khajjiar Lake at its center. Perfect for paragliding and zorbing.

Things To Do

GPO & Gandhi Chowk

Gandhi Chowk, the upper end of the town's **Mall Road**, is the bustling commercial hub. Here you'll find the main Post Office (GPO), numerous shops selling local handicrafts, woollens, and small cafes. It's the best spot for evening leisurely strolls and shopping.

Check Stores

Dainkund Peak

Known as the "Singing Hill," Dainkund is the highest peak in Dalhousie, offering spectacular **360-degree panoramic views** of the entire valley, the pine forests, and the majestic Dhauladhar ranges. It's a short, rewarding trek.
