Kedarnath Temple

The ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, part of the Chota Char Dham pilgrimage. Located at a massive altitude, it opens only for six months due to heavy snow.

Temple Darshan

Gaurikund

The base camp and starting point for the 16 km trek to Kedarnath. It is known for its natural hot water springs and is named after Goddess Parvati (Gauri).

Trek Details

Chorabari Lake (Gandhi Sarovar)

A serene glacial lake located near the temple, surrounded by majestic Himalayan peaks. It is a site of great natural beauty and spiritual tranquility.

Lake View

Mandakini River

The sacred river flowing beside the Kedarnath temple. It is one of the tributaries of the Alaknanda, adding to the purity and powerful atmosphere of the valley.

River Crossing

Himalayan Views & Peaks

The entire region is enveloped by magnificent snow-clad mountains like Kedarnath Peak, Kedar Dome, and Bhagirathi. The views are utterly breathtaking and inspiring.

Best Viewpoints

Bhairavnath Temple

Located on a hill near Kedarnath, this temple is dedicated to Lord Bhairav, believed to be the guardian deity of Kedarnath temple who protects it during the winter closure.

Guardian Deity
