Salasar Balaji Temple

This is the only temple of Hanumanji with a beard and mustache. The deity here is believed to be self-manifested, attracting millions of devotees seeking blessings and miraculous fulfillment of wishes.

Temple Information

Annual Festival and Fair

Grand fairs and festivals are held here during Hanuman Jayanti and Ashwin Purnima. These festivals showcase the vibrant culture and intense religious fervor of Rajasthan.

Festival Dates

Anjani Mata Temple, Laxmangarh

Located a short distance from Salasar, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Anjani, the mother of Lord Hanuman. Devotees often visit here after offering prayers to Balaji.

Nearby Attractions

Local Culture and Thar Desert

Salasar is situated in the Churu district of Rajasthan. Here you can experience the unique sight of Rajasthani rural life and the vast, serene landscapes of the nearby Thar Desert.

Local Experience

Pilgrim Gathering and Seva (Service)

The temple is constantly bustling with devotees, and continuous 'Akhand Kirtan' (uninterrupted chanting) takes place. The temple also provides free lodging and 'Prasad' (food) for pilgrims.

Darshan Arrangements

Khatu Shyamji Temple

Located close to Salasar, this temple is dedicated to Barbarika (grandson of Bhima), whom Krishna named 'Shyam'. A journey to Salasar and Khatu Shyamji is often taken together by pilgrims.
